Willeta Mayer (Hero)

Interest Hero

Wiletta Mayer is the main character in the play "Trouble in Mind". She's a strong and talented African American actress who isn't afraid to speak her mind. Wiletta struggles with racist stereotypes in the theater and eventually finds the courage to stand up against them.

These are other related play:

A Raisin in the Sun by Lorraine Hansberry:
A classic play about an African American family's struggles with racism and identity.
The Glass Menagerie is not related, instead Fences by August Wilson:
A play that explores the experiences of African Americans in the 1950s, dealing with themes of racism, identity, and family.
The Colored Museum by George C. Wolfe:
A satirical play that critiques the representation of African Americans in the media and explores themes of identity and racism.
Clybourne Park by Bruce Norris:
A play that explores the themes of racism, identity, and gentrification in a Chicago neighborhood.
