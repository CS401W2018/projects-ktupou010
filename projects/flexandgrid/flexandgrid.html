Tongan Handicraft

Tongan handicrafts are a beautiful and integral part of Tongan culture. They include a wide range of traditional arts such as intricate bone carving, woodcarving, fine weaving, and basket making. Two of the most important handicrafts are tapa making and the weaving of mats. These crafts showcase the pride, skill, patience, and sense of design of Tongan women

Kato Alu

The "kato alu" is a traditional Tongan basket. Unlike most ordinary baskets, which are typically woven from coconut or pandanus leaves, the kato alu is woven from the midrib of coconut leaves (tuaniu) and the aerial roots of the Epipremnum pinnatum plant (alu)

Tiki (Wood Carving)

Tongan wood carving is a traditional art form that has been passed down through generations. It involves the intricate carving of wood to create various objects, including sculptures, tools, and ceremonial items.

Kahoa (Necklace)

Tongan bone necklaces, known as "kahoa" in Tongan, are traditional pieces of jewelry that hold cultural significance. These necklaces are often handcrafted from animal bones, such as cow or whale bones, and are intricately carved to create beautiful and meaningful designs.

Kato (Bag)

The "kato loakau" is a traditional Tongan basket, similar to the "kato alu." These baskets are intricately woven and hold cultural significance in Tongan society.

Ngatu (Tapa Cloth)

Ngatu, also known as tapa cloth, is a traditional Tongan textile made from the inner bark of the paper mulberry tree. It holds significant cultural and ceremonial importance in Tongan society.

I (Fan)

Tongan fans, known as "kato alu" or "kato loakau," are traditional handwoven fans that hold cultural significance in Tongan society. These fans are crafted using natural materials such as coconut leaves, pandanus leaves, and other plant fibers.
